Before you set out with your donation, confirm

Addresses and hours change every hour. Before you travel, call the center or check its official account and confirm two things: that it's still open and that it still needs what you're bringing. On this page we mark anything from a single source we couldn't verify with ⚑ To confirm.

Bring what they actually ask for —water, non-perishable food, in-date medicine, hygiene items, diapers and infant formula—, clean and unexpired. What nobody asked for clogs things up and gets in the way. If you can, a money donation to a serious organization almost always goes further.

Take everything only to authorized centers. After a disaster, fake collection points and accounts appear and divert aid. No one legitimate charges you to help, and as Funvisis reminds us: don't be fooled by unofficial sources.

El Junquito

Collection centers in El Junquito

Read this before heading up

El Junquito was one of the hardest hit and, per the press, the State's presence there has been limited: much of the aid is moved by volunteers. They report that supplies don't always reach the highest, most remote areas (La Toma, El Hondón and beyond UNES). There will be lines and access control into the town: go up with patience. What they need most: diapers, children's clothing and feminine hygiene products.

Quick guide

How to donate well

Small habits that make your aid arrive, not get in the way.

  • Confirm before you set out. That the center is still open and still needs today what you're bringing.
  • Bring what they ask for. Water, non-perishables, in-date medicine, hygiene, diapers and formula — clean and unexpired.
  • Nothing expired or broken. Expired medicine or damaged clothing creates work, not help.
  • If you can, give money. To a serious organization it goes further and doesn't clog the roads.
  • Authorized centers only. Avoid diversion and make sure supplies reach those who need them.
  • In hard-hit areas, patience. In El Junquito and the mountains there will be lines and access control.
  • Coordinate, don't duplicate. Ask what's missing so you don't fill a center with what it already has too much of.
  • Watch out for scams. No one legitimate charges you to donate or to “speed up” aid.
